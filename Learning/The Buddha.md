### The Ash-Carved Idols (The Lighthouses of Limbo)

These statues were not placed by God, Satan, or the Church. They are the obsessive work of a single, nameless denizen of long ago—a man known only as the **Blind Carver**.
## 盲目仏刻師の物語: The Tale of the Blind Carver

The Carver felt the demonic atmosphere of his curse beginning to rot his mind, threatening to turn him into the demon [[Wrath]], the ruler of [[地獄の八輪：Violence]]. 

To stave off the madness, he began to carve statues of the Buddha to center his spirit. However, once he finishes a sculpture, he feels the face of his buddha he can tell with his fingers it's not perfect, his anger overtakes him and he does it again, cursed to continue this loop for eternity. 

## The Gameplay Mechanic (The Temporal Anchor)

Interacting with the Idol triggers the game's core checkpoint loop.

- **The Respite:** Stepping into the blinding light purges the player of damage, fully restoring health, and Heretical blood-magic meters.
    
- **The Reset (The Cost):** Drawing upon the Stagnant Grace to heal yourself resets the temporal latency of the surrounding Ring. All non-boss enemies you have violently purged from the level will instantly respawn.
    
- **The Network:** Later in the game, under [[Darzog]]'s guidance, the player learns to use the network of Idols to fast-travel, stepping through the blinding light to instantly rematerialize at previously discovered safe zones.

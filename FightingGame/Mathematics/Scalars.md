# [[Associative Property]]

--- 

### Vectors

#### The Formula

> The rule states that for any scalars  and , and any vector--you can move the scalars next to each other and multiply them first.

$$k(\vec{v})m = (k \times m)\vec{v}$$
#### Usage

$$
0.001 \times (-0.5, -0.28125, 1.0) =
0.005 \times (-0.5, -0.28125, 1.0)
$$

--- 

The mathematical law that allows this is the [associative property](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Associative_property) of scalar multiplication.

Because $0.001$ and $0.5$ are both regular numbers (scalars), you can group and multiply them together first before touching the vector.

## How It Works step-by-step

Think of your problem as multiplying three things together:

- Scalar A: $0.001$
- Vector B: $(0.5, -0.28, 1.0)$
- Scalar C: $0.5$

The rule states that for any scalars $k$ and $m$, and any vector $\vec{v}$:  
$$k(\vec{v})m = (k \times m)\vec{v}$$

You can move the scalars next to each other and multiply them first.

## The Alternative (Distributing Twice)

If you do not multiply the scalars first, you have to distribute twice. You will get the exact same answer, but with more steps:

1. Distribute the 0.001 first:  
    $$0.001 \times (0.5, -0.28, 1.0) = (0.0005, -0.00028, 0.001)$$
2. Then multiply that new vector by 0.5:  
    $$(0.0005, -0.00028, 0.001) \times 0.5 = (0.00025, -0.00014, 0.0005)$$

Multiplying $0.001 \times 0.5$ first is just a shortcut to avoid doing vector math twice.

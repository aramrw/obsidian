# Formula

The power rule for integration states that:

$${\displaystyle \int \!x^{r}\,dx={\frac {x^{r+1}}{r+1}}+C}$$

> In [calculus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Calculus "Calculus"), the **power rule** is used to differentiate functions of the form $f(x)=x^r$, whenever $r$ is a [real number](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Real_number "Real number"). 
> 
> Since [differentiation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Derivative "Derivative") is a [linear](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linearity "Linearity") operation on the space of differentiable functions, [polynomials](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polynomial "Polynomial") can also be differentiated using this rule. The power rule underlies the [Taylor series](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taylor_series "Taylor series") as it relates a [power series](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Power_series "Power series") with a function's [derivatives](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Derivative "Derivative").

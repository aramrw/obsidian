## What is Topology?

At its core, **topology** is the mathematical study of shapes and spaces, focusing entirely on the properties that survive continuous deformation. Often called "rubber-sheet geometry," it cares about the fundamental structure of an object rather than its exact size, angles, or rigid shape.

In topology, you are allowed to stretch, twist, bend, and shrink an object as much as you want. The only rules are:

1. **No tearing** (you cannot create new holes or break the object apart).
    
2. **No gluing** (you cannot fuse two separate parts together).
    

### The Classic Example: The Mug and the Doughnut

![[8c112bc6bb3135067d87243fc1b0af97.gif|165]]

To a topologist, a coffee mug and a doughnut (a torus) are exactly the same shape. If you imagine a mug made of highly flexible clay, you can squash the cup part down and stretch the material out until it seamlessly morphs into a doughnut. Because this can be done without tearing the clay or fusing new parts together, they are considered topologically equivalent.

On the other hand, you cannot turn a sphere (like a bowling ball) into a doughnut without poking a hole in it. Therefore, a sphere and a doughnut are topologically distinct.

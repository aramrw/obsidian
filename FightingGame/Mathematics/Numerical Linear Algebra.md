> **Numerical linear algebra**, sometimes called **applied linear algebra**, is the study of how [matrix operations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matrix_operation "Matrix operation") can be used to create [computer algorithms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algorithms "Algorithms") which [efficiently](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algorithmic_efficiency "Algorithmic efficiency") and accurately provide approximate answers to questions in [continuous](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Continuous_function "Continuous function") mathematics.

# Info

> Numerical linear algebra was developed by computer pioneers like [John von Neumann](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_von_Neumann "John von Neumann"), [Alan Turing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alan_Turing "Alan Turing"), [James H. Wilkinson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_H._Wilkinson "James H. Wilkinson"), [Alston Scott Householder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alston_Scott_Householder "Alston Scott Householder"), [George Forsythe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Forsythe "George Forsythe"), and [Heinz Rutishauser](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heinz_Rutishauser "Heinz Rutishauser"), in order to apply the earliest computers to problems in continuous mathematics, such as ballistics problems and the solutions to systems of [partial differential equations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Partial_differential_equation "Partial differential equation").
